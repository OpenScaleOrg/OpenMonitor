# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

OpenMonitor is maintained by OpenScale as an open-source project for the Windows community. We are committed to keeping it a welcoming, respectful, and productive space for everyone who uses or contributes to it — regardless of experience level, background, or identity.

## Expected Behaviour

- Be direct but respectful. Disagreeing with an approach is fine; dismissing people is not.
- Provide context when reporting bugs or requesting features. A clear, specific issue is a gift to the maintainer.
- Accept that decisions may not always go your way. Explain your reasoning, then let it go gracefully.
- Assume good faith. Most people filing issues or opening PRs are trying to make things better.

## Unacceptable Behaviour

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks of any kind
- Posting others' private information without consent
- Sustained disruption of discussions or deliberate trolling
- Discriminatory language or imagery

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces: GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, and any other forum where OpenMonitor community members interact.

## Enforcement

Report violations to **suman.debnath@ascentt.com**. Reports are handled confidentially. Consequences range from a warning to a permanent ban from the project, depending on severity and pattern of behaviour.
